India

What India was left with? An aftermessy life…

At the beginning of the eighteenth century, as the British historian Angus Maddison has demonstrated, India’s share of
the world economy was 23 per cent, as large as all of Europe put together. By the time the British departed India, it had
dropped to just over 3 per cent. The reason was simple: India was governed for the benefit of Britain. Britain’s rise for
200 years was financed by its depredations in India.
